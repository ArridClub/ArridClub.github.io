Club Guidelines

Overseen by the Board of Directors

The Arrid Club is committed to maintaining a safe, respectful, and welcoming space for all who walk through our doors. The following guidelines apply to all members, guests, and visitors while on Club property or attending Club-sponsored events. The Board of Directors is in full charge of the Club and is answerable to the General Membership.

Club Guidelines

Effective for all members, guests, and visitors
  • 🚫 No one under the influence of alcohol or mind-altering substances is allowed in the Club.
  • 🚫 No alcohol, drugs, or mind-altering substances are allowed in or on the premises.
  • 🤝 Respect fellow members.
  • 👶 Children under 18 are the sole responsibility of their parent or guardian and cannot be left unattended. Minors attending 12-step meetings must be supervised by an adult.
  • 📋 No literature, petitions, or solicitations may be circulated or posted without prior Board approval.
  • 👕 Shirts and shoes must be worn at all times in or on Club property.
  • 📷 To preserve anonymity, no picture-taking is allowed in or on Club property without permission.
  • 🚬 No smoking is allowed inside the Club.
  • 🔫 No weapons.
  • 🐾 Service animals only.
  • 🚲 No bikes are allowed inside the Club.
  • ♿ All handicapped parking requires proper identification.
  • 🚷 No sitting or loitering on steps, banisters, or ramps.
  • 💰 All Club monies must be placed in the safe or removed from the Club at the end of each meeting or event.
  • 7️⃣ Seventh Tradition monies collected during any meeting must not be deposited in the Arrid Club safe.
  • ☕ Cup number one (1) is always reserved for the newcomer.
Key Carriers: The Arrid Club Board of Directors requires all key carriers to have a minimum of six (6) months of clean and/or sober time. The Board reserves the right to revoke key-carrying privileges to anyone at any time.
☕

The Newcomer's Cup

Cup number one is always reserved for the newcomer. It's one of our most cherished traditions — a simple reminder that those who are new to recovery are our most important guests.
