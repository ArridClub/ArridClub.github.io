We Are Not A Glum Lot

Friendship · Laughter · Service · Community

One of the biggest misconceptions about recovery is that getting sober means giving up fun. At the Arrid Club, we know better. Recovery opens the door to real friendships, genuine laughter, and a community that shows up for each other — not just in meetings, but in life.

Life at the Arrid Club

Here's a taste of what fellowship looks like around here

🍳

Pancake Breakfasts

Saturday morning fundraisers with good food, strong coffee, and even better conversation. A favorite tradition.

🍖

BBQs

Backyard-style gatherings with food, lawn games, and the kind of easy fellowship that reminds you why you got sober.

💃

Dances

Sober dances with live music or a DJ — proof that you don't need a drink to have a great time on the dance floor.

⛳

Golf Outings

Casual rounds for all skill levels. Less about the score, more about spending a few hours outside with good people.

🎂

Anniversary Celebrations

We celebrate sobriety milestones together — from 30 days to 30 years — because every day sober is worth honoring.

🎄

Holiday Gatherings

Nobody should spend the holidays alone. Our holiday events are open to all and full of warmth, food, and fellowship.

🏕️

Campouts

Recovery under the stars. Campfire meetings, fresh air, and a reminder that the natural world is worth being sober for.

🛠️

Club Workdays

Pitching in to keep the club running. Service is a cornerstone of recovery, and there's something deeply satisfying about working alongside others.

🎤

Speaker Events

Inspiring shares from people who've been through it. Some funny, some moving, all honest — and a great place to bring newcomers.

Words That Resonate
"We are not a glum lot. If newcomers could see no joy or fun in our existence, they might not want what we have."
— Alcoholics Anonymous, The Big Book
"The joy of living is the theme of A.A.'s Twelfth Step, and action is its keyword."
— Twelve Steps and Twelve Traditions
"I used to think I needed alcohol to have fun. Sobriety showed me I just needed the right people."
— Arrid Club Member
"Recovery gave me back my life. Fellowship gave me a reason to live it fully."
— Arrid Club Member

More Than Meetings

Recovery is about more than attending meetings. Through fellowship, service, and shared experiences, we build friendships and create a community that supports lasting sobriety. You don't have to do this alone — and you don't have to do it without joy.
